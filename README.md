# Intelligent Dual-Axis Solar PV Tracking System

## Overview

This project focuses on the development and comparative study of an intelligent dual-axis solar PV tracking system designed to maximize solar irradiance capture throughout varying daylight conditions.

The project originated from an early prototype developed in 2023 using Arduino Nano, LDR sensors, and automated motor actuation. The system is now being expanded into a complete engineering portfolio project including hardware development, tracking algorithms, MATLAB simulations, analytics, and performance evaluation.

The project explores two major solar tracking methodologies:

- LDR Sensor-Based Reactive Tracking
- Time & Azimuth Angle-Based Predictive Tracking

---

## Objectives

- Improve solar irradiance capture efficiency
- Compare reactive and predictive tracking methods
- Analyze solar panel alignment behavior
- Study energy gain over fixed-tilt systems
- Develop simulation-supported tracking analysis
- Explore intelligent monitoring and fault detection concepts

---

## Tracking Methodologies

### 1. LDR Sensor-Based Tracking

This method utilizes Light Dependent Resistors (LDRs) to detect sunlight intensity differences and dynamically orient the solar panel toward the direction of maximum irradiance.

#### Features
- Real-time light sensing
- Automatic directional correction
- Dynamic sunlight tracking
- Simple control logic

---

### 2. Time & Azimuth Angle-Based Tracking

This method calculates solar position using daytime, azimuth angle, and elevation angle estimations to orient the solar panel throughout the day.

#### Features
- Predictive tracking logic
- Position-based orientation
- Reduced sensor dependency
- Improved tracking consistency
